Listening test

Two minutes to understand what Amevoca addresses.

How it works

You'll hear two versions of the same speech sample. One without intonation pitch (what many people perceive in normal conversation). One with intonation pitch restored (what Amevoca enables).

Your job: listen carefully to both and notice the difference in emotional perception. Pay attention to how your brain interprets the emotional content of each version.

What you'll discover

If your perception is typical: The difference is subtle. You'll notice the restored version sounds warmer and more emotionally present, but you already perceive the emotional content fairly well without it.

If your perception is less typical: The difference is dramatic. Without intonation pitch, the emotional content may seem neutral, cold, or even opposite from what's intended. With it restored, the actual intent becomes clear — and sometimes surprising.

Why this matters

Most people assume everyone perceives emotional content in speech the same way. This test makes visible what's usually invisible: significant variation in how brains reconstruct intonation pitch from incomplete acoustic information.

This variation isn't a deficiency. It's simply how some nervous systems work. And Amevoca is designed specifically to make up the difference.
