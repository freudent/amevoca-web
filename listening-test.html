— resonero / listening test

Can you hear what
others feel?

A short psychoacoustic test that assesses how reliably your brain reconstructs intonation melody from harmonic information. Free. Anonymous. Takes about five minutes.

headphones recommended  ·  normal hearing required  ·  no account needed

01 — before you begin

What this test measures.

The test assesses Missing Fundamental Illusion (MFI) reconstruction reliability — how accurately your auditory cortex reconstructs a fundamental pitch from its harmonics when the fundamental is absent. This is the same mechanism that determines how reliably you receive (and transmit) emotional information in speech.

Results are anonymous. We generate a reference number you can save — this lets you connect your baseline to any future results or research we publish.

For best results: Use headphones in a quiet environment. The test uses tonal pairs — you'll judge which of two tones sounds higher in pitch. There are no trick questions. Answer what you hear.

02 — the test structure

Two parts. About 5 minutes total.

The test is administered by Natasha and the Resonero team. The interactive version will be live at launch. Below is a description of what to expect.

part one — warmup

Four tone pairs with F0 present.

You'll hear pairs of tones and identify which sounds higher. In the warmup, the fundamental pitch (F0) is present in both tones. This calibrates your responses and confirms the test is working as expected.

part two — the interesting part

Four tone pairs with F0 absent.

The same task — which tone sounds higher — but now the fundamental pitch has been removed from one or both tones. Your answer reveals how your auditory cortex reconstructs the missing fundamental from harmonics alone. This is where the meaningful data is.

after the test

Your reference number and result profile.

You'll receive a reference number to save. Your result will match one of three profiles below. Results are immediate and anonymous.

03 — result profiles

What your results may suggest.

These profiles are indicative, not diagnostic. We'd rather you know before you buy.

profile A

Strong reconstruction.

Your brain reconstructs intonation melody accurately from harmonic information. Intonation Augmentation may not be what you need. If you're still experiencing communication friction, the source may be elsewhere — and the other approaches page may be more useful.

profile B

Reconstruction in an unexpected direction.

Your auditory cortex is reconstructing — but the reconstruction is systematically shifted. This is one of the profiles Resonero is designed for. The emotional signal you receive (and transmit) is present but distorted. IA delivers the undistorted version.

profile C

Reconstruction not occurring reliably.

Your auditory system is not automatically reconstructing intonation melody from harmonics. The emotional layer of speech is arriving as noise. Intonation Augmentation was built precisely for this — delivering the melody directly, bypassing reconstruction entirely.

04 — data and research

Your data supports population research.

Anonymous test results contribute to ongoing research on MFI reconstruction distribution in the general population. No personally identifiable information is collected. Your reference number is generated locally — we cannot connect it to you unless you choose to share it.

If you're a researcher interested in the dataset or in collaboration, contact info@resonero.com.
