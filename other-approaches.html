Resonero is one answer. Different people need different things. Different challenges respond to different solutions. This page exists to help you find the right fit — whether that's us or not.

01 — polyvagal approaches (e.g. forbrain)

What they do.

Polyvagal-based auditory approaches deliver modulated sound — typically via bone conduction — to engage the nervous system's stress response directly. The proposed mechanism is vagal nerve modulation: shifting the nervous system from a defensive or hypervigilant state toward a more regulated, open state.

People using these approaches report genuine benefit: easier communication, reduced anxiety, improved focus. We take these reports seriously.

How we think IA is different.

Our hypothesis is that the relevant variable isn't low-frequency sound per se — it's unidentified low-frequency sound. Harmonics without intonation melody are an unidentified low-frequency percept. An unidentified sound source can trigger nervous system caution.

Intonation Augmentation delivers intonation melody — enabling source identification. An identified source is not a threat. The nervous system stands down. This is a different mechanism from direct vagal modulation.

The broadcast voice observation: FM radio announcers use close-miced techniques capturing very high levels of low-frequency energy — far more than Resonero delivers. That broadcast voice is universally described as soothing and calming. If low-frequency sound intrinsically triggered stress, broadcast voice would be alarming. It isn't. The difference is identification: broadcast voice is a known, identified source.

when polyvagal might be right

The core challenge is nervous system stress response. You want a portable, wearable form factor. You want structured active-use sessions. IA hasn't helped.

when IA might be right

You struggle to hear or produce emotional content in speech. You experience confident misreadings. You want seamless ambient presence. Polyvagal approaches haven't helped.

We don't recommend using both simultaneously — different mechanisms, likely different populations. If one hasn't worked, the other may address something different entirely. Try separately.

02 — speech-language pathology & speech therapy

What they do.

SLPs work on production, voice quality, articulation, communication strategies, and specific communication goals. Therapy is evidence-based, clinician-guided, and targets measurable outcomes.

How therapy works — and how IA is different.

Speech therapy is fundamentally compensatory: it teaches workarounds. Speak more slowly. Enunciate more clearly. Over-explain. Read body language. Ask for clarification. These are valid strategies. They work.

Intonation Augmentation addresses a different layer: the signal itself. When intonation melody is present and clear, you don't need to compensate — you perceive and produce naturally. The goal is removing the cause of the friction, not building around it.

when SLP might be right

You want strategies, structured coaching, clinical support, or specific measurable goals. You benefit from a therapeutic relationship and expert guidance.

when IA might be right

You want to address the signal itself — not build around it. You're tired of conscious compensation. You want natural expression, not practiced technique.

These are complementary. You can use both. Bring Resonero into the conversation with your SLP — it may give them new information about what you're actually producing.

03 — acting classes & communication training

What they do.

Acting training and communication coaching teach emotion projection, voice control, body language, presence, and strategic communication. They develop a conscious toolkit for expressing yourself effectively.

How training works — and how IA is different.

Like SLP, communication training is compensatory: it builds conscious technique layered over natural expression. This can be powerful — the ability to project warmth even when you're nervous, or to hold attention in a large room.

IA operates at a different level. It enables natural emotional expression by providing accurate signal — rather than teaching you to consciously shape your voice toward a target. When the signal is clear, intention comes through naturally. No technique required.

when training might be right

You want to develop a performance skill. You want to build presence, projection, or persuasion. You enjoy the community and structured practice.

when IA might be right

You don't want to perform — you want to be yourself. You're tired of conscious thinking in every conversation. You want natural self-expression, not technique.

These can be powerful in combination: clear IA signal + conscious technique. If you're already trained and still experiencing friction, the signal layer may be what's missing.

04 — our position

Clarity, not competition.

We believe different people need different things. We believe different challenges respond to different solutions. We might be wrong about some of our hypotheses — and we want to know if we are.

If you're a researcher, clinician, or practitioner in any of these spaces — and you see an error in our reasoning, a collaboration opportunity, or a better explanation — please contact us.
