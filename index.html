Why it happens

The emotional layer of speech — intonation pitch — is physically absent at normal social distances. It dissipates within a meter of a voice. Your brain fills in what's missing, automatically and invisibly. For some people that reconstruction is accurate. For others, it's systematically distorted. They don't experience uncertainty. They experience a confident misreading that feels like the truth.

The same physics affects speakers. Without clear feedback about your own intonation pitch, you're trying to shape your voice with an instrument that isn't feeding you accurately.

How Amevoca works

Two small components placed in your room. One listens. One speaks back. Together, they deliver the intonation pitch that physics removed — so both speaker and listener finally have what they need.

No interface to manage. No mode to enter. No explanation to offer. The room simply sounds warmer. Conversations feel different. And only you know why.

Small talk carries emotional meaning

Most people feel it automatically. When someone says "cold enough for you?", they're not asking for weather analysis. They're expressing "I see you. We're okay." Most people feel that warmth directly.

For people whose intonation reconstruction is less reliable, that signal doesn't arrive. So you treat the words as a question. You answer about the weather. Meanwhile, they expected you to feel what they were feeling. The disconnect runs deep: you think they cared about weather. They think you don't care at all.

Amevoca changes this. It lets you perceive what others are trying to express. Once you know they're saying "I care" instead of asking about the weather, you can respond authentically. With whatever warmth, humor, or presence feels right to you.
