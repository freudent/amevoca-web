# Amevoca Brand Guidelines

## Logo Versions

### amevoca_logo.svg (Clean Version)
**Use this for:**
- Website headers and navigation
- Social media
- Digital displays where elegance is priority
- Favicon
- Small formats where ™ symbols clutter
- Blog posts, articles, digital marketing

**Why:** The website itself contains trademark notices in headers and footers, so the logo can display cleanly while legal protection is maintained contextually.

---

### amevoca_logo_marked.svg (Marked Version)
**Use this for:**
- Product packaging (boxes, labels)
- Device itself (if engraved/printed)
- User manuals and documentation
- Legal documents
- Brand guidelines documents
- Marketing materials where trademark claims are important
- First appearance in print advertising
- Brand identity guidelines

**Why:** Establishes trademark ownership clearly in contexts where legal notice is essential and where there's no surrounding legal language.

---

## Trademark Notices

### Primary Marks
1. **Amevoca™** — Product name and brand
2. **Love your voice™** — Brand promise and positioning

### When Both Appear Together
"**Amevoca™: Love your voice™**"

### Footer Legal Notice (on all web pages)
"Amevoca™ and 'Love your voice'™ are trademarks of Freudensong LLC."

### Etymology
From Latin *ame* (love) + *voca* (voice) = love your voice

---

## Logo Components

- **Waveform symbol** — Unmarked (represents frequency/intonation visually)
- **"amevoca" wordmark** — Marked with ™ in marked version
- **"love your voice" tagline** — Marked with ™ in marked version

---

## Design Notes

### Color
- Primary text: #1a1a1a (dark gray/black)
- Waveform: #AFA9EC (light purple) and #7F77DD (purple)
- Tagline: #7F77DD (purple)

### Spacing
- Logo should have clear space around it (minimum 1/2 the height of "amevoca" text)
- Never crowd the logo with other text or elements

### Sizing
- Minimum size: 100px wide for web
- On devices: Scale appropriately to hardware constraints
- On packaging: Prominent placement, minimum 1 inch

---

## Brand Voice
The brand promise is that **others can finally hear your care**. 

- Not about self-love or self-improvement
- About relational clarity — emotional authenticity in communication
- Not clinical; human and warm
- Emphasizes both speaker clarity and listener understanding
